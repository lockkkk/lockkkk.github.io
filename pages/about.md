Hi, I’m Jiaxin Lin. I’m a Computer Science PhD student at the University of Texas at Austin. I am part of the [University of Texas Networked Systems (UTNS)](https://utns.cs.utexas.edu/) Lab. I am broadly interested in **computer networks**, **systems** and **architecture**, especially in programmable network devices, cloud computing, and data center networks. The overarching goal of my research is to design new software, hardware, communication protocols, and system abstractions that efficiently utilize the semantics and capabilities provided by advanced network hardware and interconnects.


I was a recipient of [2024 MIT EECS Rising Star](https://risingstars-eecs.mit.edu/participants/?y=2024&search_page=2), [2023 Google Ph.D. Fellowship](https://research.google/outreach/phd-fellowship/recipients/) and [2021 Meta Ph.D. Fellowship](https://research.fb.com/fellows/lin-jiaxin/).

**Current research directions include:**
- Host-accelerator interfaces over next-generation interconnects (e.g., CXL, chip-to-chip links).
- Programming frameworks and compilers for in-network computing ([NSDI'25](https://www.usenix.org/conference/nsdi25/presentation/lin-jiaxin), [MICRO'23](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3613424.3614291)).
- Architecture and applications of programmable network devices ([NSDI'23](https://www.usenix.org/conference/nsdi23/presentation/lin), [OSDI'20](https://www.usenix.org/conference/osdi20/presentation/lin), [SIGMETRICS'23](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3589980)).
- Systems support for robust, secure, and manageable heterogeneous cloud infrastructure.
